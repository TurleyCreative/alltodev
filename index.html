Scene 01 · Site Boundary

The Red Line

This is the defined application boundary for the proposed development — commonly referred to as the 'red line' boundary.

The site sits at the edge of Manchester's Northern Quarter, approximately 0.3 hectares of brownfield land bounded by Oldham Street to the west and a service lane to the east.

The red line defines the full extent of land within the planning application. All proposed works, access arrangements, and land ownership fall within this boundary.

Scene 02 · Wider Context

Setting & Surroundings

Zooming out to understand the site within its neighbourhood and city context.

The site is located 400m north-east of Piccadilly Gardens and within the Northern Quarter Conservation Area. The surrounding area is characterised by Victorian brick warehouses, independent businesses, and a high footfall pedestrian network.

Key infrastructure — Piccadilly Station, Metrolink, and the A62 — all fall within 600m. The site benefits from exceptional connectivity.

Scene 03 · Study Area

The Study Area

This animated boundary highlights the wider study area considered as part of this consultation — the zone within which impacts, opportunities, and connections have been assessed.

The study area extends approximately 500m from the site boundary, encompassing key streets, public spaces, and heritage assets that may be affected by or benefit from the proposed development.

This boundary is not a planning designation — it is a working area used to frame the evidence base and consultation scope.

Scene 04 · Constraints & Proposals

Constraints & Opportunities

Several planning constraints and opportunities shape what can be proposed on this site. Click any zone on the map, or the cards below, to explore each one.

Planning Constraint
Conservation Area
The site falls within the Northern Quarter Conservation Area. New development must preserve or enhance the area's character.
Opportunity
Public Realm Improvement
A 6m public realm strip along the street frontage creates an opportunity for a high-quality pedestrian environment.
Access
Servicing & Access Route
Primary access must be taken from the rear service lane. The Oldham Street frontage is to remain pedestrian-only.

Scene 05 · 3D Visualisation

The Proposal in Context

The map now shows a 3D aerial view with building heights extruded, so you can understand the proposed massing in relation to its surroundings.

The proposed development rises to 7 storeys at its tallest point — stepping down to 4 storeys toward the conservation area to respect the established street hierarchy.

The camera will slowly orbit the site. Use the map controls to pause rotation, or drag to explore manually.
